Football

MTG runs a comprehensive football for girls’ program in the coastal region that entails leagues and tournaments involving over 200 primary and secondary schools annually. The MTG United Team comprises elite players who participate in national and international matches, thereby positioning girls for professional football and expanding their career and livelihood pathway.

HOW WE DO IT

  • March officials training: We train girls and young women on coaching, refereeing and first aid services at various levels.

  • Life skills: Information about HIV & AIDS, nutrition, water, sanitation and hygiene are integrated into football training sessions and football drills.

  • Leaders training: These include field representatives who directly manage the playing fields.

  • Tournaments: We organize primary school tournaments, secondary school tournaments, and annual women's tournament, which are aimed at showcasing and nurturing girls and young women football talents at all levels.

  • MTG Football League and Champions League: The leagues run from March to October. All the other programs are anchored onto the football league.

  • MTG United team. The best players from each field are selected to form the MTG United team which represents the organization at various tournaments.

Education

The program aims at improving the education outcomes of girls and young women in the coastal region, by increasing opportunities for girls to enrol, remain in school and complete their education.

HOW WE DO IT

  • Scholarships: We offer scholarships support to vulnerable girls and young women so they can afford to pay for their education.

  • Awards: We recognize the commitment and pay school fees for our most active members through an award scheme.

  • Resources: At our Resouce Center, we welcome learners to enjoy our free study materials and supportive space to learn. We also reach out to the community with our mobile library, so that everyone can access our books.

  • Goal Setting: We guide our members to set achievable goals and devise a strategy to access further education or pursue their career objectives.

  • Mentorship: We believe everyone has the ability to achieve — they just need the right support network. We boost the confidence and self-belief of our members through regular motivational talks and mentorship workshops.

    • Livelihood



    The program aims at assisting young women in their pathway towards financial independence.

    MTG facilitates skills transfer in financial literacy and entrepreneurship, access to capital through savings groups and financial institutions, business mentorship and incubation services through MTG’s Acceleration Center.

    This is coupled with work readiness interventions that entail the development of career pathways, role modelling and motivational talks for girls, linkages with Technical and vocational Institutions, internships and employment opportunities.

    Health

    MTG leverages football to equip girls with knowledge, skills, products and services to make informed decisions regarding their sexual and reproductive health. This is aimed at reducing teenage and unwanted pregnancies amongst young girls and thus positioning them to complete their primary and secondary education.

    HOW WE DO IT

    1. Reproductive health sessions:These peer-led sessions are school-based and also conducted during the leagues, tournaments and major events.
    2. HIV testing and counselling services.
    3. Breast and cervical cancer screening.
    4. Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM).
    5. Counselling sessions to our beneficiaries and the community.

    Leadership

    Considering the patriarchal nature of the coastal community, leadership roles either social, economic or political are solely taken by males.

    Our Leadership program is modelled around football as we believe sport has the power to transform and build leaders.

    Girls and young women learn by doing. Girls and young women are provided potential space to pursue authority over particular social, economic and political relations.

    HOW WE DO IT

    Leadership training: We conduct leadership trainings such as Trainer of Trainers (T.O.T), Capacity building and residential trainings.

    Girls and young women run and manage the MTG leagues.

    YMEP

    “Without the involvement of men and boys in gender equality and elimination of Gender-Based Violence is like a doctor treating symptoms and ignoring the disease."

    In expanding safe spaces for young women and girls in the community, MTG has intensified male engagement and is working actively with young boys and men as allies to influence norms, attitudes and behaviours towards promotion and respect of girls and women’s rights.

    This is achieved through sensitization of boys and young men, male parents, local leaders and influential male leaders on gender, positive masculinity, responsible fatherhood and SRHR.

    HOW WE DO IT

    • Training: We conduct training sessions on positive masculinity for boys and young men.
    • Sensitization meetings: We target fathers, male guardians and influential male leaders through sensitization forums on gender, positive masculinity, responsible fatherhood and SRHR.

    Child Protection

    At the heart of MTG programming, is child protection and safeguarding that is fully integrated into its entire operations to ensure safety to all beneficiaries while participating in MTG activities is guaranteed.

    The program aims at safeguarding all children involved in our activities from harm, abuse and neglect in, around and through sport by providing a safe environment for play.

    HOW WE DO IT

  • Sensitization and capacity building of our staff members, volunteers and stakeholders on child protection and safeguarding.
  • Risk assessments: Based on a risk matrix these are done before any activities to access possible risks and plan ahead on how to mitigate them.
  • Follow-up on child protection concerns in the communities that we serve.
